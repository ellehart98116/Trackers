Selenium in the Elk Valley: sources, ecology, and environmental response

A systems map tracing Se mobilization from coal mining → environmental chemistry → food-web transfer → biological effects → regulatory response and restoration

The Elk Valley (British Columbia) is one of the most studied examples of selenium loading from coal mining into an aquatic watershed. Coal mining exposes selenium-bearing rock, which leaches into the Fording and Elk Rivers, enters the food web, and accumulates in fish and wildlife. This map traces the full pathway from source to ecological effect to regulatory and restoration response, organized left to right. Cite badges link to primary sources for further reading.
① Elk Valley sources & mobilization
Geology & site context
Coal-bearing formations, Elk Valley
Fording River coalfield — selenium naturally elevated in coal seams and overburden; mining exposes and oxidizes Se-bearing rock
Environment Canada — Selenium PSL Assessment Report
Teck Coal / Elk Valley Resources — 5 mines
Fording River, Greenhills, Coal Mountain, Line Creek, Elkview — large open-pit operations; majority of Se loading to watershed; now owned by Glencore subsidiary
Teck Sustainability Reports (annual)
Se mobilization pathways
Waste rock & spoil leachate
Oxidation of Se-bearing minerals in waste rock → selenate-rich leachate → direct discharge to tributaries of Fording & Elk Rivers
BC Ministry of Environment — Elk Valley Water Quality Plan (2014)
Active mine drainage
Pit water and contact water management; drainage routed to creeks; historically untreated; treatment infrastructure under construction 2017–present
BC Elk Valley Water Quality Plan (BC MoE, 2014)
Transboundary transport — Koocanusa Reservoir
Elk R. → Lake Koocanusa (MT/BC) → Kootenai R. (MT/ID) → Snake/Columbia basin; cross-border Se loading documented by IJC & USGS
IJC Koocanusa Monitoring & Assessment Report (2020)
Five open-pit coal mines mobilize selenium from coal-associated rock into the Fording and Elk Rivers — the foundational fact of all Elk Valley litigation
② Se chemistry & water quality
Speciation in Elk Valley waters
Selenate dominance in mine drainage
Oxidized mine drainage → selenate (SeO₄²⁻) dominant; highly mobile; travels to Lake Koocanusa; converted to organic Se by algae/biofilm downstream
USGS WRI 01-4147 — Se speciation, Kootenai R. basin
Biofilm conversion → selenomethionine
Periphyton in Fording/Elk Rivers assimilate selenate → organoselenium (SeMet); primary driver of food-web bioaccumulation; toxicity multiplied
Lemly 2002 — Se cycling in streams (Environmental Toxicology & Chemistry — verify issue)
Regulatory water quality thresholds
Canadian water quality guidelines — Se
CCME guideline: 1 µg/L (freshwater aquatic life); routinely exceeded in Elk Valley tributaries by 5–20×; basis for Fisheries Act enforcement
CCME Canadian Water Quality Guidelines — Selenium (2009)
US EPA freshwater Se criterion (2016)
Fish tissue criterion: 15.1 µg/g dw (egg/ovary), 8.5 µg/g dw (whole body); water column: 3.1 µg/L lotic; applied in MT and ID downstream of Elk Valley
US EPA — Aquatic Life Criteria for Selenium (2016)
BC provincial WQOs — Elk Valley
Site-specific objectives set by BC MoE; Teck / EVR required to meet targets by 2028; enforced through Environmental Management Act permits
BC Elk Valley Water Quality Plan (2014)
Water Se concentrations in Elk Valley routinely exceed Canadian and US guidelines — but tissue and food-web data are the stronger litigation evidence
③ Food-web transfer & bioaccumulation
Selenate → biofilm/periphyton uptake
Large bioconcentration from water (100–1000×); Se incorporated into algal tissue as SeMet; primary trophic entry point in Fording/Elk Rivers
Hamilton 2004 — Se in aquatic food webs (Science of the Total Environment — verify)

Invertebrate bioaccumulation
Chironomids, mayflies, stoneflies, amphipods accumulate Se from periphyton; tissue Se 3–10× above food source; primary dietary exposure route for westslope cutthroat trout
Adams et al. 2003 — Environmental Toxicology & Chemistry 22(9):2020–2029

Fish dietary Se accumulation
Westslope cutthroat trout (Species at Risk) accumulate Se through invertebrate diet; egg Se elevated; larval deformities documented; whole-body Se monitored under EVWQP
Environment Canada — Elk Valley aquatic effects monitoring (ECCC)
↓
Piscivorous bird exposure
Osprey, heron, merganser, bald eagle; dietary Se from contaminated fish; potential egg effects; documented in some Kootenai/Columbia basin assessments
Ohlendorf 2003 — avian Se toxicology review (ETC — verify citation)
The food-web pathway — water → biofilm → invertebrates → cutthroat trout (Species at Risk) — is the central ecological harm pathway in Elk Valley litigation
④ Documented biological harm
Fish — primary litigated receptor
Elevated fish tissue Se — Elk Valley
Westslope cutthroat trout whole-body and egg Se regularly exceed EPA tissue criteria (15.1 µg/g dw egg; 8.5 µg/g dw whole body); documented in monitoring reports
BC Elk Valley WQP & annual Teck AEMP monitoring reports
Larval deformities & reproductive failure
Spinal, craniofacial, fin deformities in trout larvae; edema; hatch failure; consistent with Se embryotoxicity framework; ecologically significant in a listed species
Adams et al. 2003 — ETC 22(9):2020–2029
Westslope cutthroat trout — Species at Risk
Listed under Canada's Species at Risk Act (SARA); Se-caused reproductive harm = potential SARA violation; amplifies Fisheries Act liability; U.S. ESA may apply in MT/ID
COSEWIC — Westslope Cutthroat Trout Status Report (2016)
Aquatic ecosystem
Invertebrate community impairment
Reduced invertebrate diversity and abundance in impacted reaches; community shifts documented in AEMP; indirect effect on fish food supply and ecosystem function
Teck Aquatic Effects Monitoring Program (annual, 2012–present)
Koocanusa / Kootenai R. exceedances
Lake Koocanusa Se has increased markedly over past two decades; documented exceedance of US EPA criterion; IJC monitoring confirms transboundary loading
IJC Koocanusa Monitoring & Assessment Report (2020)
Westslope cutthroat trout is both the primary ecological receptor and a Species at Risk — combining Fisheries Act and SARA exposure in Canadian litigation
⑤ Supporting toxicology science
Foundational field evidence
Kesterson precedent — Ohlendorf et al. 1986–87
First documented field case of Se-caused reproductive failure in aquatic birds; established that Se from anthropogenic drainage = legally cognizable ecological harm
Ohlendorf et al. 1986 — Science 233:1067–1068
Heinz et al. — dose-response lab evidence
Controlled mallard feeding studies; maternal transfer to eggs; SeMet >> inorganic Se toxicity; EC values for embryo mortality; foundational for threshold derivation in litigation
Heinz et al. 1992 — ETC 11(4):405–408 (verify issue)
Threshold science — avian eggs
Fairbrother et al. 1999 ANCHOR
Egg Se concentrations as predictors of avian toxicity; compiled field/lab data; proposed effect thresholds for birds; widely cited in ecological risk assessment and environmental standard-setting
Fairbrother et al. 1999 — Human & Ecological Risk Assessment 5:1229–1253
Fairbrother et al. 2000 ANCHOR
Defense of threshold methodology vs. Skorupa critique; addresses field confounder concerns; threshold robustness analysis; important methodological reference for Se risk assessment
Fairbrother et al. 2000 — Human & Ecological Risk Assessment 6:203–212
Adams et al. 2003 ANCHOR
EC10/EC20 modeling of field+lab avian Se data; quantified effect concentrations; defensible dose-response framework; widely used in ecological risk assessment and environmental standard development
Adams et al. 2003 — Environmental Toxicology & Chemistry 22(9):2020–2029
Ecosystem conceptualization
Lemly — ecosystem Se conceptual framework
Food-web based risk assessment; tissue residues as primary metric; dietary TRVs; Se hazard quotient; ecosystem-scale conceptual framework for watershed-level Se assessment
Lemly 1993 — ETC 12(4) & Lemly 2002 — Ecotoxicology & Environmental Safety (verify)
The toxicology literature (Ohlendorf → Heinz → Fairbrother/Adams → Lemly) traces the full causal chain from Se exposure to quantified ecological harm — a foundation for risk assessment and environmental decision-making
⑥ Policy, regulation & enforcement
Canadian federal — Fisheries Act
Fisheries Act s. 36(3) — harmful substance
Prohibits deposit of deleterious substances into waters frequented by fish; selenium from mine drainage has been assessed under this provision; primary federal environmental protection mechanism
Fisheries Act, RSC 1985, c F-14, s 36(3) (Justice Canada)
Teck Coal — $60M Fisheries Act penalty (2017)
Largest-ever Fisheries Act penalty at the time; acknowledged deposit of selenium into Elk Valley watershed; $58M directed to Ktunaxa Nation-led watershed restoration program
The Regional — Fisheries Act penalty & Ktunaxa restoration fund
2023 EVR enforcement — $3.6M penalty
Penalty for delays in water treatment construction and exceedance of Se/nitrate limits at multiple mine sites; $1.5B+ treatment infrastructure investment committed; ongoing regulatory monitoring
Elk Valley Resources / EVR compliance disclosures; BC MoE enforcement records
Species at Risk Act
SARA — westslope cutthroat trout
Westslope cutthroat trout listed under SARA; Se-caused reproductive impairment of a listed species triggers additional federal protections and recovery planning obligations
Species at Risk Act, SC 2002, c 29 (Justice Canada)
Cross-border & transboundary frameworks
1909 Boundary Waters Treaty — IJC oversight
Canada-US treaty framework for shared water bodies; IJC Koocanusa monitoring program documents transboundary Se loading; binational coordination on water quality objectives
International Joint Commission — Boundary Waters Treaty resources
US water quality — MT, ID downstream waters
US EPA freshwater Se criteria apply to Kootenai River reaches in Montana and Idaho; USFWS and state agencies monitor fish tissue; Endangered Species Act (bull trout, burbot) adds federal layer
US EPA — Natural Resource Damages under CERCLA
The 2017 Fisheries Act penalty and the resulting Ktunaxa Nation restoration fund are among the most significant environmental accountability outcomes in BC mining history
⑦ Indigenous rights & restoration
Ktunaxa Nation
Ktunaxa Nation — primary First Nations partner
Traditional territory encompasses Elk Valley watershed; fish central to cultural and subsistence practices; Ktunaxa involved in EVWQP development; co-lead on $58M restoration fund
Ktunaxa Nation Council — official website & Elk Valley resources
Crown duty to consult — s. 35 Constitution Act
Section 35 protects Aboriginal rights including fisheries; Crown and industry have consultation obligations with Ktunaxa on decisions affecting their territory and fisheries; foundational to co-management arrangements
Haida Nation v BC [2004] 3 SCR 511 — foundational duty to consult
UNDRIP — Indigenous environmental rights
Canada adopted UNDRIP (2016); BC DRIPA (2019) — BC bound to align laws with UNDRIP; articles on environmental protection of Indigenous lands/waters; emerging legal basis for Ktunaxa claims
UN Declaration on the Rights of Indigenous Peoples (2007)
Restoration & treaty rights
$58M Ktunaxa restoration fund
From 2017 Fisheries Act fine; Ktunaxa Nation-led; targets native fish habitat, Se mitigation, watershed restoration; first Nations-led environmental fund of this scale in BC
The Regional — Ktunaxa restoration fund details
Aboriginal fisheries rights — Se harm nexus
Harm to fish = harm to constitutionally protected right to fish; Se-caused reproductive failure reduces fish populations available for exercise of rights; substantive legal nexus to injury claim
R v Sparrow [1990] 1 SCR 1075 — foundational Aboriginal fisheries rights
The Ktunaxa Nation are both a rights-holder and a restoration co-manager — the 2017 outcome set a precedent for Indigenous-led environmental remediation funded by corporate fines
⑧ Regulatory & management response
BC provincial — Elk Valley Water Quality Plan
Elk Valley Water Quality Plan (2014)
BC MoE-required plan; sets Se & nitrate objectives for each mine; requires water treatment, adaptive management, reporting; compliance milestone: 2028 targets; basis for ongoing enforcement
BC Ministry of Environment — Elk Valley WQP (2014)
Se water treatment — $1.5B+ investment
Biological Se reduction (SRB reactors), ion exchange, constructed wetland treatment; Line Creek, Elkview, Fording River facilities; technology still maturing; litigation risk persists if targets not met
Teck / EVR Sustainability Reports — water treatment (annual)
Monitoring frameworks
Aquatic Effects Monitoring Program (AEMP)
Required under Fisheries Act authorizations; fish tissue Se, invertebrate communities, reproductive endpoints; data used in enforcement actions and litigation; annual public reporting

IJC Koocanusa monitoring program
