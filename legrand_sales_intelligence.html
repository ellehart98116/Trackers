Conversation Starters
Opening Moves
Hook — Current Events
"How are you thinking about barrels given the Tronçais supply situation?"
Opens with something real and current. Shows market awareness. Transitions naturally to LeGrand's secured allocations and forest relationships as the solution, not a sales pitch.
Hook — Vintage Outlook
"With this warm spring in Napa, are you thinking about harvest timing — and how does that affect your barrel program?"
Links weather (something they care about intensely) to barrel planning. Warm vintage = more structured tannins = barrel selection matters more, not less. Creates relevance.
Hook — Ratings / Market
"Your 2022 scored really well. What's the barrel strategy for the 2023 and 2024 programs?"
Flattery plus genuine curiosity. High-scoring producers are protective of their barrel program and open to conversations about how to repeat or improve results.
Hook — New Producers
"Congratulations on the launch — what's your oak vision for the debut vintage?"
Fresh producers have no incumbent to displace and are eager for guidance. Position LeGrand as a collaborative partner, not just a vendor. Offer a consultation, not a catalog.
Objection Handling
Responses
Objection: "You're too expensive"
Acknowledge, then reframe: cost per barrel is only part of the math. The real question is what a point improvement in score is worth — at $50/bottle, 94 vs. 93 is meaningful margin. Also: competitor prices just went up $18–22 this year. "I understand. Let me ask — what would one extra point on your critic score translate to in wholesale price? That's usually the better math than per-barrel cost."
Objection: "We're happy with our current supplier"
Don't fight it. Ask for a trial. A single trial barrel in a new lot costs less than $300 and could change everything. "Totally understood — and I don't want to displace something that's working. But would you be open to a trial barrel on your next experimental lot? You'd have the comparison in the glass."
Objection: "We're moving toward neutral oak / less oak"
This is an ONYX Volcanic opportunity, not a loss. Volcanic process = integration without imposition. "That's exactly why ONYX Volcanic exists — it's not about adding oak flavor, it's about what happens to texture and mouthfeel when you use energy transfer without combustion. Very different tool."
Objection: "Lead times are too long"
Turn into urgency. Supply is tight. Early commitments get allocation. "That's actually why I'm here now — if you wait until July, Tronçais fine grain won't be available for fall delivery. We're taking harvest commitments through June."
LeGrand Differentiators
Value Props
3-Generation Traceability
From log selection to stave aging to cooperage — LeGrand controls and documents every step
No other importer offers this level of chain of custody. In an era of sustainability claims and certification requirements, this is a verifiable, meaningful differentiator. Ask if their winery is doing any sustainability certification — they almost all are, and this story helps them.
ONYX Volcanic — Unique Technology
Superheated stone energy transfer — no combustion, no smoke artifacts, pure oak expression
Nothing else on the market does this. It's not a gimmick — UC Davis pilot data shows measurably different volatile phenol profile. Best target: winemakers who love oak texture but worry about smoke/char overtopping fruit.
J.M. Gonçalves — Five Toast Styles
24-month French & American oak available in five distinct toast profiles for program precision
Breadth of options means a winemaker can create a tailored blend across toast levels rather than committing to one profile. Great for experimental or multivarietal programs. Gonçalves is the accessible entry point; Signature is the upgrade path.
Marc Le Grand Signature — Extra Fine Grain
36 and 48-month outdoor seasoning, three extended toast regimes — for producers who want science on their side
Supported by peer-reviewed research showing astringency reduction from extended seasoning. Target: 93+ point producers who want to move to 95+. Price premium is a feature, not a bug — it signals commitment.
