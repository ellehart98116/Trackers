Draft Contaminant Candidate List 6 (CCL 6) — PFAS as a class
Pre-rulemaking · public comment open
EPA Office of Water · SDWA § 1412(b)(1)(B)
EPA's draft CCL 6 (Federal Register, April 6, 2026) includes PFAS as a class as one of four chemical groups identified for possible future drinking-water regulatory consideration under SDWA. EPA 2026 Interim Guidance on Destruction & Disposal of PFAS
Interim guidance · non-binding
EPA OLEM · Updated 2026 from 2024 baseline
EPA's non-binding interim guidance identifying lower-release options for managing PFAS-containing materials. The 2026 update focuses on three commercially available technology categories: permitted Class I underground injection wells for fluids; permitted RCRA Subtitle C hazardous-waste landfills where landfill disposal is selected and PFAS concentrations are relatively high; and thermal treatment / incineration under certain conditions, while flagging continued concerns about PFAS emissions as products of incomplete destruction. The guidance does not establish requirements. Open for public comment through June 29, 2026. Pairs with the DoD 2026 Interim Guidance for installation cleanup planning.

RCRA Listing — 9 PFAS as Hazardous Constituents
Final-rule stage · pending publication
EPA OLEM · 40 CFR 261 Appendix VIII
Proposes adding PFOA, PFOS, PFBS, HFPO-DA, PFNA, PFHxS, PFDA, PFHxA, PFBA (and salts/structural isomers) to Appendix VIII as hazardous constituents (not hazardous wastes). Would not by itself impose cradle-to-grave hazardous-waste controls but would affect RCRA corrective action at TSDFs — EPA identified ~1,740 potentially affected facilities. The Unified Agenda listed an April 2026 final-rule target; the final rule has not yet been published in the Federal Register. Biggest near-term remediation lever.

Window locked · substantive rule pending
EPA OPPT · 40 CFR 705

Biosolids Sewage Sludge Risk Assessment (PFOA/PFOS)
Draft · pending finalization
EPA OW · CWA Section 405
Draft assessment, not enforceable limits. EPA released the Draft Sewage Sludge Risk Assessment for PFOA/PFOS on Jan 14, 2025; public comment period closed Aug 14, 2025. EPA found that modeled human-health risks may exceed acceptable thresholds in some scenarios at 1 ppb PFOA or PFOS in sewage sludge land-applied at typical agronomic rates. Final timeline uncertain. Any future regulation would come after the final risk assessment, and would be under CWA § 405 if warranted — could inform future risk-management actions and POTW effluent guidelines.

PFAS National Primary Drinking Water Regulation (NPDWR)
Final
EPA Office of Water · 40 CFR 141 · Final April 10, 2024
Enforceable MCLs: PFOA & PFOS at 4.0 ppt; PFHxS, PFNA, HFPO-DA at 10 ppt; Hazard Index = 1 for PFHxS/PFNA/HFPO-DA/PFBS mixture. EPA plans to retain PFOA/PFOS NPDWRs while extending compliance deadlines (proposed extension to 2031), and to rescind/reconsider PFHxS, PFNA, HFPO-DA, and the Hazard Index mixture. Under D.C. Circuit challenge in AWWA v. EPA; outcomes could affect the scope, timing, or continued validity of some or all PFAS NPDWR requirements.
Implementation support: EPA's PFAS OUT initiative will proactively contact ~3,000 water systems with known PFOA/PFOS challenges, beginning with systems above 4 ppt in summer 2026. PFAS OUT connects systems to funding and technical-assistance resources but does not itself provide direct funding or technical assistance.

CERCLA Hazardous Substance Designation
Final · Retained
EPA OLEM · 40 CFR 302.4 · Final April 19, 2024
PFOA and PFOS designated as CERCLA hazardous substances. Triggers reportable quantity (1 lb) release notifications and enables Superfund cost recovery. EPA confirmed in 2025 it will retain the designations. EPA's Enforcement Discretion and Settlement Policy states that EPA will focus CERCLA enforcement on major contributors and does not intend to pursue certain passive receivers — such as water utilities, municipal airports, local fire departments, municipal landfills, and farms — where equitable factors do not support CERCLA response actions or costs.

Completed phase-out · monitoring
FDA · Voluntary phase-out (2020 announcement)
FDA announced in February 2024 that grease-proofing materials containing PFAS are no longer being sold for use in food packaging in the U.S., fulfilling the voluntary commitment from the 2020 announcement. Affected coated paper, paperboard, and bakery liners. FDA continues food-surveillance testing and related market monitoring.

FDA Total Diet Study & Survey Testing
Ongoing
FDA Center for Food Safety & Applied Nutrition
Ongoing market-basket and Total Diet Study surveys measuring PFAS in foods. Dec 19, 2025 release: 6 additional regional collections published (14 total now posted), with detectable PFAS in 7.2% (39/542) of samples; highest concentrations in shrimp, clams, catfish, and tilapia. Cumulative since 2019: 95% of 1,352 samples have no detectable PFAS. May 2026 infant formula market basket affirmed "undetectable or very low levels" across 16 brands. Results inform FDA food-surveillance priorities, dietary-exposure assessment, and future FDA activities.

TSCA PFAS Risk Management, Testing & SNUR Actions
In progress
EPA OPPT · multiple TSCA authorities
EPA is using TSCA authorities to address PFAS in commerce, including SNURs for inactive and long-chain PFAS, the National PFAS Testing Strategy, and a TSCA §6 proceeding related to PFAS formed during fluorination of plastic containers. Pairs with TSCA 8(a)(7) reporting — the dataset from reporting will improve EPA's view of PFAS manufacture, import, and use across product categories.

CPSC PFAS Research & Materials
Research / watch item
Consumer Product Safety Commission
CPSC has PFAS research, exposure, hazard, and risk-assessment materials for non-food consumer products (carpets, textiles, juvenile products, household goods) under CPSA authority. No major PFAS-specific federal consumer-product ban or rule is currently comparable to state product phaseouts — CPSC's chemicals page lists research and "next steps" materials, not a PFAS-specific consumer-product rule. Worth monitoring as state activity continues.

🏷️ State product laws — see State watchlist
State-level PFAS-in-products laws affect product stewardship directly. The 19-state watchlist covers Maine (38 MRS §1614), Minnesota (Amara's Law), California (Prop 65 + multiple product-category bans), New York (apparel + food packaging), Colorado (HB22-1345 + SB24-081), Washington (Safer Products WA), Vermont, New Mexico (PFAS Protection Act), Rhode Island (Consumer PFAS Ban Act), and other states with active product bans, MCLs/action levels, cleanup standards, or biosolids requirements — deadlines and effective dates are in those cards.

DWSRF Emerging Contaminants Set-Aside
Active · state-administered
EPA · State-administered · BIL appropriation
$4B over FY22–FY26 via Bipartisan Infrastructure Law for Drinking Water SRF Emerging Contaminants funding — primarily PFAS. State-administered funding for eligible drinking-water EC projects (timing varies by state). FY26 capitalization grants begin April 2026, ending Feb 2027. Up to 31% of grant available as set-asides.

CWSRF Emerging Contaminants
Active through FY2026
EPA · Clean Water SRF · BIL appropriation
$1B over FY22–FY26 via Bipartisan Infrastructure Law for emerging contaminants — including PFAS — in wastewater, stormwater, and nonpoint-source pollution. State-administered. Relevant to biosolids, POTW upgrades, stormwater systems, and watershed-scale PFAS work. Pairs with the federal Biosolids Risk Assessment.

TEC RealWaterTA (Tackling Emerging Contaminants)
Active · technical assistance
EPA · No-cost technical assistance · not direct funding
EPA's no-cost technical-assistance program for small or disadvantaged drinking-water systems addressing PFAS and other emerging contaminants. Supports sampling plans, PFAS NPDWR monitoring, preliminary engineering / alternatives analysis, funding applications, and public outreach. Does not provide direct funding.

SDC-EC Grant (Small & Disadvantaged Communities)
Open
EPA · Noncompetitive to states/territories · BIL appropriation
$5B over FY22–FY26 ($1B annually); awarded noncompetitively to states and territories, who use funds for eligible small or disadvantaged communities to address emerging contaminants — primarily PFAS — in drinking water. No cost-share/match. Strong fit for clients with under-funded water systems in PFAS-impacted areas.

EPA Brownfields Grants (PFAS-eligible)
Annual cycle · current cycle closed
EPA · Multipurpose, Assessment, Cleanup, RLF
Brownfields grants have been PFAS-eligible since 2024. Multipurpose ($800K), Assessment ($500K community-wide), Cleanup ($500K per site), and Revolving Loan Fund. Annual NOFO cycle; EPA's funding page reports no open or upcoming opportunities at this time. Useful when PFAS is one contaminant of concern among many.

DoD Environmental Restoration Account
Active
DoD · FY26 NDAA · Site-driven
Per GAO-25-107401, DoD estimates $9.3B+ in PFAS investigation & cleanup needs beyond FY25. Funds investigations and remedial actions at the 723 identified sites; not competitive but the funding pool driving DoD CERCLA contractor work.

FEMA BRIC (PFAS / resilience overlap)
FY24–FY25 NOFO active · FY26 TBD
FEMA · Building Resilient Infrastructure & Communities
BRIC funds resilience projects; water-system projects with PFAS or contaminant-resilience components have been funded in past cycles. FEMA announced a FY2024–FY2025 BRIC NOFO on March 25, 2026, making $1B available. FY26 NOFO timing TBD. Worth tracking for clients pairing PFAS treatment with broader resilience upgrades.

Open
USDA · Rural Decentralized Water / SEARCH
