Pre-rulemaking · public comment open

EPA's draft CCL 6 (Federal Register, April 6, 2026) includes PFAS as a class as one of four chemical groups identified for possible future drinking-water regulatory consideration under SDWA. EPA 2026 Interim Guidance on Destruction & Disposal of PFAS
Interim guidance · non-binding
EPA OLEM · Updated 2026 from 2024 baseline
EPA's non-binding interim guidance identifying lower-release options for managing PFAS-containing materials. The 2026 update focuses on three commercially available technology categories: permitted Class I underground injection wells for fluids; permitted RCRA Subtitle C hazardous-waste landfills where landfill disposal is selected and PFAS concentrations are relatively high; and thermal treatment / incineration under certain conditions, while flagging continued concerns about PFAS emissions as products of incomplete destruction. The guidance does not establish requirements. Open for public comment through June 29, 2026. Pairs with the DoD 2026 Interim Guidance for installation cleanup planning.

RCRA Listing — 9 PFAS as Hazardous Constituents
Final-rule stage · pending publication
EPA OLEM · 40 CFR 261 Appendix VIII
Proposes adding PFOA, PFOS, PFBS, HFPO-DA, PFNA, PFHxS, PFDA, PFHxA, PFBA (and salts/structural isomers) to Appendix VIII as hazardous constituents (not hazardous wastes). Would not by itself impose cradle-to-grave hazardous-waste controls but would affect RCRA corrective action at TSDFs — EPA identified ~1,740 potentially affected facilities. The Unified Agenda listed an April 2026 final-rule target; the final rule has not yet been published in the Federal Register. Biggest near-term remediation lever.

Biosolids Sewage Sludge Risk Assessment (PFOA/PFOS)
Draft · pending finalization
EPA OW · CWA Section 405
Draft assessment, not enforceable limits. EPA released the Draft Sewage Sludge Risk Assessment for PFOA/PFOS on Jan 14, 2025; public comment period closed Aug 14, 2025. EPA found that modeled human-health risks may exceed acceptable thresholds in some scenarios at 1 ppb PFOA or PFOS in sewage sludge land-applied at typical agronomic rates. Final timeline uncertain. Any future regulation would come after the final risk assessment, and would be under CWA § 405 if warranted — could inform future risk-management actions and POTW effluent guidelines.

PFAS National Primary Drinking Water Regulation (NPDWR)
Final
EPA Office of Water · 40 CFR 141 · Final April 10, 2024
Enforceable MCLs: PFOA & PFOS at 4.0 ppt; PFHxS, PFNA, HFPO-DA at 10 ppt; Hazard Index = 1 for PFHxS/PFNA/HFPO-DA/PFBS mixture. 🆕 May 1, 2026: OMB completed interagency review of EPA's proposed NPDWR amendments — Federal Register publication imminent. Proposed amendments: retain PFOA/PFOS NPDWRs and 4 ppt MCLs, rescind regulatory determinations for PFHxS, PFNA, HFPO-DA, and the Hazard Index mixture, and extend compliance from 2029 to 2031. Under D.C. Circuit challenge in AWWA v. EPA; outcomes could affect the scope, timing, or continued validity of some or all PFAS NPDWR requirements.
Implementation support: EPA's PFAS OUT initiative will proactively contact ~3,000 water systems with known PFOA/PFOS challenges, beginning with systems above 4 ppt in summer 2026. PFAS OUT connects systems to funding and technical-assistance resources but does not itself provide direct funding or technical assistance.

CERCLA Hazardous Substance Designation
Final · Retained
EPA OLEM · 40 CFR 302.4 · Final April 19, 2024
PFOA and PFOS designated as CERCLA hazardous substances. Triggers reportable quantity (1 lb) release notifications and enables Superfund cost recovery. EPA confirmed in 2025 it will retain the designations. EPA's Enforcement Discretion and Settlement Policy states that EPA will focus CERCLA enforcement on major contributors and does not intend to pursue certain passive receivers — such as water utilities, municipal airports, local fire departments, municipal landfills, and farms — where equitable factors do not support CERCLA response actions or costs.

PFAS Use At Data Centers Emerges As New Litigation Focus, Attorney Says
LitigationMay 14, 2026InsideEPA · PFAS News
How data centers use PFAS: two technically distinct pathways — fluorinated cooling gases (atmospheric release; GWP thousands × CO&sub2;) and PFAS immersion-cooling fluids (potential water-pathway release). U.S. Chamber of Commerce 2024 report identified data centers as one of 7 critical PFAS-reliant industry sectors, but disposal and wastewater pathways have received little study. Source & signal: Eve Gartner (Earthjustice) flagged data centers as a next-wave PFAS site category during a May 13 Environmental Law Institute webinar launching ELI's March 2026 report "Current Trends in Toxics Litigation." Implications for science work: new site type for environmental site assessment, fate-and-transport modeling, and PFAS characterization; expect demand for source-attribution and exposure-pathway analyses. EPA announced last year it's prioritizing TSCA new-chemical reviews for data-center and AI projects — though unclear if existing PFAS in cooling get the same scrutiny. State of the field: ELI's Cecilia Diedrich predicts PFAS litigation will surpass asbestos in scope and cost; causation (long latency, science gaps) remains the central scientific question. Forward-looking: CIEL's Patrick Boyle expects microplastics to follow the same trajectory.

EPA's New Biosolids Guide May Tier Risks Based On PFAS Concentrations
📌 PinnedFederal PolicyMay 11, 2026InsideEPA · PFAS News
OW Assistant Administrator Jessica Kramer announced May 7 (U.S. WASH Convening, D.C.) that EPA is developing new guidance on PFAS in biosolids — explicitly framed as correcting confusion from the Biden-era PFOA/PFOS draft risk assessment, which industry, states, farming groups, and water systems criticized for overestimating risk via non-real-world modeling. New guidance will use "real-life scenarios" and signals a tiered-concentration approach aligned with Michigan, Minnesota, Virginia, and Maryland (NACWA's model template: ≥100 µg/kg PFOA+PFOS triggers land-application ban + source-reduction program; ≥50 µg/kg triggers source-reduction + reduced application rate of 3 dry tons/acre). The Biden draft RA comment period closed August 2025; EPA is still reviewing comments. Practical signal: directional shift toward a less restrictive federal posture on biosolids land application, prioritizing the highest-concentration material. Pinned: foundational evergreen reference — will shape state programs, remediation cost-recovery, and any future federal biosolids rulemaking.

OMB Completes Interagency Review of EPA's Proposed PFAS NPDWR Amendments
📌 PinnedFederal PolicyMay 1, 2026InsideEPA · Federal Regulatory
OMB cleared EPA's proposed NPDWR amendments on May 1, 2026 — the proposed rule is now poised for Federal Register publication. Substance of the proposal (per Morgan Lewis summary): retain PFOA and PFOS NPDWRs and MCLs at 4 ppt, rescind regulatory determinations for PFHxS, PFNA, HFPO-DA, and the Hazard Index mixture, and extend the compliance deadline from 2029 to 2031. Why this matters for scientific staff: directly affects every Integral drinking-water PFAS engagement — monitoring program design, treatment selection, system-by-system compliance scoping, and risk communication assumptions. The Hazard Index rescission removes the multi-PFAS mixture framework that some state programs were aligning to; cleanup-level decisions referencing the rescinded determinations need to be re-grounded. Maine — Title 38 §1614

Phased bans across multiple effective dates. Minnesota Pollution Control Agency filed a new complaint May 1, 2026 alleging incomplete remediation of Cottage Grove PFAS contamination; seeks $30,000/day civil penalties. Emerging theory: data centers as defendants
May 14, 2026Novel theory of liability
Data centers are being publicly flagged as a next-wave PFAS site category. Why this matters for scientists: data centers use PFAS in two technically distinct ways — fluorinated cooling gases (atmospheric release; GWP thousands × CO&sub2;) and immersion-cooling fluids (potential water-pathway release). New site type for environmental site assessment, fate-and-transport modeling, and PFAS characterization work. Full coverage in InsideEPA News.
