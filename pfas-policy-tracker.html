Active federal rules 8

PFAS National Primary Drinking Water Regulation (NPDWR)
Final
EPA Office of Water · 40 CFR 141 · Final April 10, 2024
Enforceable MCLs: PFOA & PFOS at 4.0 ppt; PFHxS, PFNA, HFPO-DA at 10 ppt; Hazard Index = 1 for PFHxS/PFNA/HFPO-DA/PFBS mixture. EPA proposed extending compliance from 2029 to 2031; final extension expected Spring 2026. EPA intends to rescind/reconsider the four non-PFOA/PFOS standards. Under D.C. Circuit challenge in AWWA v. EPA — a vacatur would reset MCLs nationwide.

CERCLA Hazardous Substance Designation
Final · Retained
EPA OLEM · 40 CFR 302.4 · Final April 19, 2024
PFOA and PFOS designated as CERCLA hazardous substances. Triggers reportable quantity (1 lb) release notifications and enables Superfund cost recovery. EPA confirmed in 2025 it will retain the designations. Enforcement Discretion Policy still narrows liability for water utilities, airports, farms, and passive receivers.

RCRA Listing — 9 PFAS as Hazardous Constituents
Final expected April 2026
EPA OLEM · 40 CFR 261 Appendix VIII
Adds PFOA, PFOS, PFBS, HFPO-DA, PFNA, PFHxS, PFDA, PFHxA, PFBA (and salts/isomers) to Appendix VIII. Enables corrective action at ~1,740 TSDFs; does not impose cradle-to-grave hazardous waste controls. Watch for the Final Rule notice — biggest near-term remediation lever.
Watch FR for final rule
EPA RCRA proposal page →
TSCA Section 8(a)(7) PFAS Reporting
Under revision
EPA OPPT · 40 CFR 705
Requires anyone who manufactured/imported PFAS 2011–2022 to report identity, uses, volumes, exposure, disposal. EPA proposed scope changes Nov 2025 — the substantive 8(a)(7) revision is still in proposed-rule stage, not finalized. Submission window now starts Jan 31, 2027 (or 60 days post final-rule effective date). Most submitters' deadline: Oct 13, 2026; small-business article importers: April 13, 2027.
EPA TSCA 8(a)(7) page →
Biosolids Sewage Sludge Risk Assessment (PFOA/PFOS)
Draft · pending finalization
EPA OW · CWA Section 405
Draft Sewage Sludge Risk Assessment released Jan 14, 2025 evaluating PFOA/PFOS in land-applied biosolids, surface disposal, and incineration. Public comment period closed Aug 14, 2025. Draft found human-health risks exceeding EPA thresholds at PFOA/PFOS concentrations of just 1 ppb in biosolids land-applied at typical agronomic rates. Does not yet establish enforceable limits; final timeline uncertain. Once finalized, will inform future risk-management actions and effluent guidelines for POTW PFAS discharges.

EPA 2026 Interim Guidance on Destruction & Disposal of PFAS
Active · Pinned
EPA OLEM · Updated 2026 from 2024 baseline
EPA's operative federal posture on destroying and disposing of PFAS-containing wastes. Maintains support for high-temperature incineration at permitted hazardous-waste incinerators while flagging continued concern about PFAS emissions as products of incomplete destruction. Authorizes hazardous-waste landfills and deep-injection wells; addresses emerging technologies. Pairs with the DoD 2026 Interim Guidance for installation cleanup planning.

State watchlist 13 states

Top states whose PFAS rules drive remediation decisions — stricter MCLs, biosolids limits, broad product bans. Refreshed weekly with the federal content.

Maine — Title 38 §1614
Active
Broadest state PFAS law in the U.S.
Jan 1, 2026: sale ban on cleaning products, cookware, cosmetics, dental floss, juvenile & menstrual products, ski wax, upholstered furniture, and most textiles. May 25, 2026: plant-fiber packaging ban. Jan 1, 2032: full ban on intentionally added PFAS unless designated “currently unavoidable use.” Sludge land-application heavily restricted.
Maine DEP PFAS in Products →
Minnesota — Amara's Law
Active
Sweeping PFAS ban with phased deadlines
Reporting deadline extended to Sept 15, 2026 — manufacturers must report each PFAS-containing product sold in MN. Jan 1, 2032: full ban on intentionally added PFAS unless CUU. Initial product-category bans already in effect.
MPCA PFAS reporting →
California
Active
Prop 65 + product-specific bans
Prop 65 PFAS listings drive labeling/litigation. Active bans on: food packaging, juvenile products, cosmetics, textiles/apparel (AB 1817), cookware disclosures. Drinking water response/notification levels stricter than federal for some PFAS.

New York
Active
Drinking water + product bans
MCLs PFOA 10 ppt, PFOS 10 ppt (in effect since 2020). Food packaging PFAS ban since Dec 2022. Jan 1, 2026: children's products ban. 2028: firefighting PPE PFAS ban.

New Jersey
Active
Earliest state MCLs · sludge limits
MCLs PFOA 14 ppt, PFOS 13 ppt, PFNA 13 ppt (set 2018–2020, predate federal). 2024 PFAS-in-products law: cookware labeling, juvenile-product ban. Active biosolids/sludge investigations.

Massachusetts
Active
Sum-of-6 drinking water standard
MCL of 20 ppt for sum of 6 PFAS (PFOA, PFOS, PFNA, PFHxS, PFHpA, PFDA) — one of the strictest practical compliance standards in the country. Affects cleanup-level decisions at remediation sites.

Vermont
Active
Early adopter of MCLs
MCL of 20 ppt for sum of 5 PFAS (PFOA, PFOS, PFNA, PFHxS, PFHpA). Act 36 product bans on food packaging, ski wax, rugs/carpets, aftermarket stain/water resistance, firefighting PPE.

Connecticut
Action level
Tightest action level in U.S.
Drinking water action level of 2 ppt (combined PFOA/PFOS) — lowest in the country. Note: an action level is a non-enforceable trigger for response and notification, not an enforceable MCL like other states have set. Practical effect for utilities is similar but the legal mechanism differs. PFAS Action Plan covers AFFF takeback, food packaging ban, and ongoing site investigations.

Funding & grants PFAS-eligible federal programs

Federal funding pipelines that PFAS work can tap. Useful for client communications and proposal scoping. Status: Open = current cycle accepting · Forthcoming = announced, opening soon · Closed = current cycle closed, next TBD.

DWSRF Emerging Contaminants Set-Aside
Open
EPA · State-administered · BIL appropriation
$5B over FY22–FY26 via BIL for emerging contaminants in drinking water — primarily PFAS. FY26 capitalization grants begin April 2026, ending Feb 2027. Up to 31% available as set-asides. Eligible: public water systems serving disadvantaged and small communities.

SDC-EC Grant (Small & Disadvantaged Communities)
Open
EPA · Competitive · BIL appropriation
$5B over FY22–FY26; competitive grants directly to public water systems serving small/disadvantaged communities. No matching requirement. Strong fit for clients with under-funded water systems in PFAS-impacted areas.

EPA Brownfields Grants (PFAS-eligible)
Annual cycle
EPA · Multipurpose, Assessment, Cleanup, RLF
Brownfields grants have been PFAS-eligible since 2024. Multipurpose ($800K), Assessment ($500K community-wide), Cleanup ($500K per site), and Revolving Loan Fund. Annual NOFO cycle. Useful when PFAS is one contaminant of concern among many.
