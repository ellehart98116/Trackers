How to use the PFAS Regulatory & Industry Tracker

Practical usage guidance for the curated reference and its weekly digest
← Back to the tracker

About this guide

This guide describes practical ways to use the PFAS Regulatory & Industry Tracker and its companion weekly email digest. The tracker is a curated reference covering federal, state, international, litigation, and industry PFAS developments. Three usage modes are described below: daily check-in, weekly catch-up, and project-specific lookup.

For inclusion criteria, editorial workflow, AI use, citation policy, and disclaimers, see the About & Methodology tab on the tracker.

Three usage modes

Daily check-in (one minute)

Open the tracker. Scroll to the top of the Regulatory Reference tab and check the Recent updates box. This shows the most recent additions and revisions, organized by category (Federal, InsideEPA News & Legal, State, International, Reference Shelf). Each entry links directly to the underlying card or section.

For a faster scan: read just the Trending now callout. Topics are listed reverse-chronologically by date added, so the newest items are at the top.

Weekly catch-up (five minutes)

The simplest path is to read the Monday-morning email digest, which summarizes the previous week’s additions and updates in the same category order as the Recent updates box.

If reading the tracker directly: open the Regulatory Reference tab, scan Recent updates and Trending now, then check Federal Key Dates Ahead for upcoming compliance deadlines within the next 60 days.

Subscribe to the digest via the Subscribe to weekly updates button on the tracker, or by emailing Ellen Hartley with name, role, and group.

Project-specific lookup (variable)

Use the Table of Contents to navigate directly to the relevant section. The tracker has four major content areas:

  • Federal Actions — EPA, DoD, federal-agency rules, programs, and enforcement priorities
  • State Watchlist — state-level laws, MCLs, action levels, biosolids, product bans (19 states currently tracked, with filter pills by topic)
  • International Overlay — EU, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, Belgium, Brazil (10 international cards)
  • Product Stewardship — FDA, CPSC, food packaging, consumer-product PFAS

Use the industry filter pills at the top of Federal Actions to narrow cards by industry segment (biosolids, AFFF, drinking water, industrial wastewater, etc.). The Live FR Stream at the top of the Regulatory Reference tab pulls real-time Federal Register notices via the federalregister.gov public API.

For news context, switch to the Latest News tab, which surfaces InsideEPA coverage and Litigation Coverage from the past 30 days.

Navigation tips

Tabs

The tracker has four tabs:

  • Regulatory Reference — the main curated content (federal, state, international, product stewardship, Reference Shelf)
  • Latest News — InsideEPA News and Litigation Coverage from the past 30 days
  • Submit Content — mailto-based form for submitting items to be considered for inclusion
  • About & Methodology — inclusion criteria, editorial workflow, AI use disclosure, source citation policy, disclaimers

Cross-references

Many cards include cross-reference links to related cards or sections elsewhere in the tracker. Clicking a cross-reference will switch tabs automatically if the destination lives in a different view.

External links

Links to external sources (outside the tracker) are marked with a small ↑ arrow icon. Some external links require subscription access (InsideEPA articles are marked with a lock icon 🔒). Integral employees can access InsideEPA via the companywide subscription documented at The Hive → Library Services → Company Subscriptions.

Citing tracker content

When citing facts, regulatory developments, or court rulings referenced in the tracker in professional analysis or deliverables, cite the underlying primary source (Federal Register notice, court order, agency publication, legislative text, etc.) rather than the tracker itself. The tracker functions as a discovery and navigation aid; the underlying primary sources remain the authoritative reference. Direct links to original sources are provided with each entry where available.

Submitting content

Use the Submit Content tab on the tracker to suggest an item for inclusion. Submissions are reviewed against the same curation gate documented on the About & Methodology tab. Items that pass the gate appear in the next weekly update; items that do not pass are not added but may be referenced for future context.
